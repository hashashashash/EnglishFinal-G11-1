Theme Analysis

Moral Ambiguity and Utopia

We can say that the story's main theme is the moral ambiguity of the idealistic happiness of the many, which rests on the suffering of the few. Utopian societies are often built upon the misery of the stragglers. Le Guin describes Omelas as a paradise, "bright-towered by the sea", with processions, music, and "boundless and generous contentment", yet this supposed utopia is built on a child's "abominable misery" (Le Guin), which the citizens of Omelas are fully aware of, and that is what makes their suffering the "wholly" (Le Guin) necessary condition for prosperity. This dictates the terms of their injustice: the ceaseless child’s misery must persist for their unjust comfort to continue. This illustrates the false nature of their "innocent" happiness. Omela's satisfaction is not a sign of true happiness; it is a sign of a transaction. The suffering of the child is a cost paid to the citizens by the child for their "innocent" happiness, which is not, and can never be true happiness. The range of responses (acceptance, grief, and exile) illustrates the utopia of Omelas is built on the suffering of the child.

The Child

The child in The Ones Who Walk Away from Omelas is the moral/symbolic essence of this story—a confusing, overlooked figure (“it could be a boy or a girl”) locked in a black, dirty cellar, starved down to emaciation, festering sores, and desperate pleas (“I will be good. Please let me out”). This child is emblematic of every oppressed people who suffer in silence in order for society to experience bliss, as Omelas is a world where everything is indeed “perfect.” The pain this child endures, including lost memories of sunlight and a gradual loss of the capacity for speech, is the harsh reality of the cost of this “perfection,” challenging the people in Omelas to live with this contradiction in paradise. The child is also a metaphor for every marginalized populace who suffers in silence for the gain of a society living in bliss, including a catalyst in the story as inspirations for people who “walk away.”
